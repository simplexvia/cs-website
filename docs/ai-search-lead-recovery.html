Why your leads are declining — and why more blog posts won't fix it

Legal searches now trigger an AI-generated overview 78% of the time — the highest rate of any search category — and searchers click through to a website only 8% of the time when one appears, versus 15% when it doesn't.1 That gap is why the tactics that built law-firm SEO for a decade are losing effectiveness. Recovering leads means becoming the trusted, citable answer — not publishing more pages.

Old ModelNew Model
Rank for broad legal termsBecome the trusted answer for a specific legal problem and jurisdiction

Optimize only the firm websiteBuild a consistent firm and attorney presence across trusted sources
Use anonymous contentShow responsible attorneys, credentials, review dates, jurisdiction

Is this the right fit for your firm?

This program works best for a specific kind of firm. Here's how to tell before you reach out.

Good fit

  • 2–30 attorneys relying on local or regional search for new matters
  • Noticeable decline in calls, forms, or consultations
  • An established website with real historical traffic and defined practice areas
  • Willing to discuss which matters and practice areas are actually profitable
  • An attorney who can serve as content and compliance approver
  • Looking for a durable authority strategy, not a quick ranking trick

Let's talk it through first

  • You want guaranteed rankings or guaranteed cases — no vendor can promise that, including us
  • Lead quality or retained-matter data can't be shared or discussed
  • You're picturing a chatbot that gives visitors individualized legal advice
  • You need hundreds of near-duplicate location pages rather than fewer, stronger ones
  • No attorney is available to review and approve content or claims
